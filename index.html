  • The Concept and Motivation
    A kitchen holds so many appliances that function to different things. Let's say I want to reheat chicken, I could choose the microwave, oven, air fryer, and many other choices. The choice of the appliance is based on how we want the food to be made or reheated in, such as crispy, soft, hard, soggy. We want to remake the microwave to accommodate all of the different appliances into one.

    Our motivation to make a smart microwave was to decrease the amount of appliances in the kitchen to reduce the space they take up and also make using a microwave more efficient in today's world. With so many technological advances, we want to combine a lot of things we use in our daily lives, such as Alexa, to change the way our lives look in the kitchen.
  • Existing approaches
    There have been progression in the microwave since it's creation to make it more "smart" and also other appliances such as an air fryer. A lot of the features we want to implement in the smart microwave doesn't exist yet in that capacity. One of the microwaves I have found is the Toshiba ML-EC42P which has some of the features we want to implement, but not all.
  • Prototype
    We have already created a cardboard prototype of our smart microwave. Some of the new ways we plan on making a more functional smart microwave is creating an upgraded physical model with and interegrating the technologies we want or making a virtual prototype to allow for a bigger range of possible functionalities.
  • Five Technical Challenges

    Some of the features we want to implement into the smart mirowave are:
    -Connectivity with Alexa/Google Home
    -Vapor Vent
    -Water proof glass
    -High temperature isolation material
    -Metal Detector
    -Weight system
    -Self Cleaning

    With so many ideas and with the time frame of the project, we will have to narrow down which we want to choose. After narrowing down, we will also have to figure out which ones are doable for us to do at our scale and knowledge.

    With making a physical prototype, we can allow the possible users and viewers an easier way to understand all of the features that the smart microwave has. If we opt for the virtual prototype, we can add more features that we would like, but it will be more time consuming creating the model and we also have a lack of knowledge of how to do virtual models. With that, it will also be time consuming to research and learn as we construct.
