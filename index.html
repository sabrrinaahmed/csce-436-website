  • The Concept and Motivation
    A kitchen holds so many appliances that function to different things. Let's say I want to reheat chicken, I could choose the microwave, oven, air fryer, and many other choices. The choice of the appliance is based on how we want the food to be made or reheated in, such as crispy, soft, hard, soggy. We want to remake the microwave to accommodate all of the different appliances into one.

    Our motivation to make a smart microwave was to decrease the amount of appliances in the kitchen to reduce the space they take up and also make using a microwave more efficient in today's world. With so many technological advances, we want to combine a lot of things we use in our daily lives, such as Alexa, to change the way our lives look in the kitchen.
  • Impact Statement
    Through our production of the smart microwave, we want to revolutinize what a kitchen will look like in the future. We want to deduce clutter, streamline cooking, and intergrate technologies we use every day. Through the use of smart devices like an smartphones and google homes, it will give people the ability to use a microwave from wherever they are. Like a mother heating up their children's food from work to not having to get out of bed in the morning to make breakfast, we will give people the ability to make cooking easier, add less oversight, and efficient.

  • Five Technical Challenges

    Some of the features we want to implement into the smart mirowave are:
    -Connectivity with Alexa/Google Home
    -Vapor Vent
    -Water proof glass
    -High temperature isolation material
    -Metal Detector
    -Weight system
    -Self Cleaning
    -Alert System

    With so many ideas and with the time frame of the project, we will have to narrow down which we want to choose. After narrowing down, we will also have to figure out which ones are doable for us to do at our scale and knowledge.

    With making a physical prototype, we can allow the possible users and viewers an easier way to understand all of the features that the smart microwave has. In the time frame and knowledge level, we aren't going to be able to make an actual working smart microwave.

    If we opt for the virtual prototype, we can add more features that we would like, but it will be more time consuming creating the model and we also have a lack of knowledge of how to do virtual models. With that, it will also be time consuming to research and learn as we construct.

    Usually when you defrost something in the microwave, it tells you to input the weight of the food. We want to integrate that into the microwave. The issue we have found with the idea is that you have to weigh the dish and then add the food and weigh again. The microwave will have to do the math to find the weight difference. We will have to allow for some storage system for it to remember the last weight and do the calculations.

    The idea sounds like a useful tool, but integrating it into the microwave can be difficult or costly. It will be hard for us with the lack of knowledge to implement this in a physical way.

    With the alert system, the idea is to send a message to the user in case of an emergency, like fire. With doing so, we will need to find a way to connect the microwave to possibly the internet.
